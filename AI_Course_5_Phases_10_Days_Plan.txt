AI Course Plan for IPS Officer
Delivery Model: 5 Phases | 10 Days per Phase | 1 Hour 30 Minutes per Day | 5 Days per Week
Instructional Mix per Session: 30% Theory | 50% Hands-On | 20% Discussion
Overall Design Approach: Foundations to advanced, hands-on focused, professional usage first, then government / police / cyber / defence adaptation, then governance and audit readiness.
Tooling Principle: Mostly free / freemium tools, with primary emphasis on ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, and other accessible productivity tools.

Recommended Daily Session Structure (90 Minutes)
- 25 minutes: Concept and demonstration
- 45 minutes: Guided hands-on exercise
- 20 minutes: Discussion, reflection, scenario critique, and takeaways

==================================================
PHASE 1 — UNDERSTAND AI
==================================================

Phase Purpose
This phase builds the learner’s core mental model of Artificial Intelligence. It is designed to remove hype, clarify capability versus limitation, explain how modern AI systems work at a practical level, and establish the baseline understanding needed for safe and effective usage in later phases.

Day 1: What Artificial Intelligence Is and Why It Matters
Description: Introduces Artificial Intelligence in practical terms, explains where it appears in everyday and professional life, and establishes the difference between automation, analytics, and AI. The session focuses on building a realistic understanding rather than a buzzword-driven one.
Hands-On Focus: Exploring and comparing basic tasks in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and DeepSeek to observe how AI systems respond to simple prompts and general questions.

Day 2: Traditional Software vs Artificial Intelligence
Description: Explains the difference between rule-based software and pattern-learning systems. The learner develops a practical understanding of why AI behaves probabilistically rather than deterministically.
Hands-On Focus: Running the same task in structured software-like instruction form versus open natural language prompting to observe different behavior styles.

Day 3: Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Generative AI
Description: Clarifies the relationship between Artificial Intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, and generative AI. This session helps the learner organize the field into understandable layers.
Hands-On Focus: Mapping example tools and use cases into the correct category, such as classification, generation, summarization, translation, and image creation.

Day 4: How Large Language Models Work at a High Level
Description: Explains in simple language how large language models generate responses, why they sound fluent, and why fluency must not be confused with correctness.
Hands-On Focus: Prompt experiments showing how output quality changes with context, task framing, and follow-up questioning.

Day 5: What Artificial Intelligence Can Do Well
Description: Covers strong use cases such as summarization, rewriting, ideation, drafting, classification, translation, extraction, question-answering, and structured thinking assistance.
Hands-On Focus: Performing the same professional tasks across multiple models and comparing strengths by task type.

Day 6: What Artificial Intelligence Cannot Reliably Do
Description: Focuses on limitations including hallucination, false confidence, missing context, poor judgment, factual unreliability, and sensitivity to weak prompting.
Hands-On Focus: Deliberately testing AI on ambiguous, incomplete, and factual tasks to observe failure modes.

Day 7: Understanding Text, Image, Audio, and Video AI
Description: Gives a practical overview of multimodal AI capabilities and explains how modern AI systems now work across multiple content forms.
Hands-On Focus: Text prompting, image generation or analysis, voice or transcription examples, and capability comparison across available free / freemium tools.

Day 8: How Professionals Use Artificial Intelligence in Daily Work
Description: Introduces real professional usage patterns across corporate environments such as email drafting, report creation, research support, presentation preparation, meeting summarization, and document refinement.
Hands-On Focus: Creating a realistic productivity workflow using ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and DeepSeek for one end-to-end professional task.

Day 9: How to Evaluate Artificial Intelligence Output
Description: Teaches the learner how to read AI output critically, identify weak reasoning, spot likely fabrication, and assess whether an output is usable, partially usable, or unsafe.
Hands-On Focus: Reviewing model outputs, marking errors, and improving them through follow-up instructions.

Day 10: Integrated Foundation Lab
Description: Consolidates all foundational learning into one practice-driven session. The learner demonstrates understanding of what AI is, how it behaves, where it is useful, and where caution is required.
Hands-On Focus: A guided mini-project involving research, summarization, drafting, critique, and output comparison across tools.

==================================================
PHASE 2 — USE AI SAFELY
==================================================

Phase Purpose
This phase trains the learner to use AI responsibly, critically, and securely. It builds discipline in prompting, validation, privacy-aware usage, bias awareness, human oversight, and safe handling of sensitive information.

Day 1: Introduction to Safe and Responsible AI Usage
Description: Establishes why AI safety matters for professionals, administrators, and officers. This topic frames responsible usage as part of everyday competence, not as an advanced specialist concern.
Hands-On Focus: Reviewing example prompts and outputs to identify safe, unsafe, weak, and high-risk usage patterns.

Day 2: Prompting Fundamentals for Reliable Output
Description: Teaches the mechanics of giving clear instructions to AI systems, including role, task, context, format, constraints, and expected outcome.
Hands-On Focus: Rewriting weak prompts into stronger prompts and testing quality improvements across tools.

Day 3: Structured Prompting for Professional Work
Description: Covers task structuring methods for summarization, drafting, review, analysis, comparison, tabulation, and action planning.
Hands-On Focus: Building reusable prompt templates for office work, research support, and content refinement.

Day 4: Validation and Cross-Checking
Description: Explains why AI output must be verified, how to validate claims, and how to triangulate between model outputs, source documents, and human judgment.
Hands-On Focus: Cross-checking the same output using multiple models and source-based verification.

Day 5: Hallucination and Fabrication
Description: Focuses deeply on how AI can generate false information, invented references, incorrect legal or factual statements, and misleading summaries.
Hands-On Focus: Prompting AI into known failure scenarios and then designing correction workflows.

Day 6: Privacy, Confidentiality, and Sensitive Information
Description: Introduces data sensitivity, personal data risk, operational confidentiality concerns, and why prompts must be designed carefully in real-world work.
Hands-On Focus: Converting unsafe real-world tasks into privacy-aware prompt patterns and redacted workflows.

Day 7: Bias, Fairness, and Framing Risk
Description: Explains how bias can arise from data, prompt framing, output assumptions, and selective context. The learner develops awareness of fairness-related issues in interpretation and generation.
Hands-On Focus: Comparing outputs generated from differently framed prompts and identifying bias signals.

Day 8: Human Oversight and Decision Responsibility
Description: Reinforces that AI assists human work but does not remove human accountability, especially in official, legal, administrative, or sensitive contexts.
Hands-On Focus: Role-play exercises where the learner must decide what to accept, revise, reject, or escalate from an AI-generated output.

Day 9: Safe AI Workflow Design
Description: Teaches how to build repeatable and safe workflows for document handling, research support, reporting, and communication without over-dependence on AI.
Hands-On Focus: Designing stepwise workflows with clear checkpoints for review, validation, and escalation.

Day 10: Responsible Usage Simulation Lab
Description: Concludes the phase through realistic scenarios that require prompting, checking, redacting, validating, and finalizing AI-assisted outputs.
Hands-On Focus: End-to-end simulation combining productivity, safety, privacy, and review discipline.

==================================================
PHASE 3 — APPLY AI IN PROFESSIONAL WORK
==================================================

Phase Purpose
This phase shows how modern professionals use AI in practical day-to-day work. The learner develops operational fluency in writing, analysis, productivity, research, presentations, data handling, and workflow support using mostly free / freemium AI tools.

Day 1: AI for Writing and Rewriting
Description: Covers practical usage for emails, briefs, notes, executive summaries, formal communication, and refinement of tone and clarity.
Hands-On Focus: Drafting and improving official-style and professional communication in multiple tones.

Day 2: AI for Reading and Summarizing Documents
Description: Teaches the use of AI to reduce reading time while preserving accuracy and context.
Hands-On Focus: Summarizing long documents, extracting key points, and preparing briefing notes from uploaded content.

Day 3: AI for Research and Information Structuring
Description: Shows how AI can support initial research, topic mapping, concept explanation, and question generation while still requiring human verification.
Hands-On Focus: Building research notes, extracting structured insights, and generating follow-up questions.

Day 4: AI for Reports, Minutes, and Briefings
Description: Focuses on converting raw notes or fragmented inputs into organized outputs such as reports, meeting minutes, status updates, and briefing formats.
Hands-On Focus: Turning rough content into polished, structured documents.

Day 5: AI for Presentations and Public Communication Drafts
Description: Covers idea development, presentation outline creation, message simplification, and adaptation of content for different audiences.
Hands-On Focus: Building a small presentation outline and public-facing communication draft using AI assistance.

Day 6: AI for Tables, Categorization, and Basic Data Support
Description: Introduces non-technical use of AI for information grouping, categorization, comparison tables, extraction fields, and spreadsheet-friendly structuring.
Hands-On Focus: Creating comparative tables, classification outputs, and structured summaries from messy input.

Day 7: AI for Translation, Simplification, and Multilingual Communication
Description: Shows how AI can help convert technical language into plain language and support multilingual adaptation for broader communication.
Hands-On Focus: Translating and simplifying content into clearer professional language and alternate language forms where available.

Day 8: AI for Productivity Workflows
Description: Teaches chaining of tasks such as reading, summarizing, drafting, refining, and formatting into one repeatable work process.
Hands-On Focus: Completing one full productivity workflow from raw input to final professional output.

Day 9: Multi-Tool Comparison and Tool Selection by Task
Description: Helps the learner understand that different tools may perform better for different types of tasks such as writing, analysis, long-form thinking, document handling, or concise output.
Hands-On Focus: Performing the same task in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, and selected freemium support tools, then choosing the best fit by use case.

Day 10: Professional Productivity Capstone
Description: Integrates the phase into one realistic assignment involving drafting, research, summarization, communication, comparison, and final output preparation.
Hands-On Focus: End-to-end professional use case completed with human review checkpoints and discussion of efficiency gains.

==================================================
PHASE 4 — APPLY AI IN GOVERNMENT / POLICE / CYBER / DEFENCE CONTEXT
==================================================

Phase Purpose
This phase adapts general and professional AI capability to sensitive public-sector, policing, cyber, and defence-oriented contexts. The goal is not blind adoption but informed use with realism, caution, and operational relevance.

Day 1: AI Use Cases in Government and Public Administration
Description: Introduces practical use cases such as citizen communication drafts, policy note support, document review, record summarization, awareness content, translation, and service support.
Hands-On Focus: Designing AI-assisted workflows for administrative and public communication tasks.

Day 2: AI for Office Work in Government Settings
Description: Covers the practical day-to-day office use of AI in official environments, while discussing sensitivity, review discipline, and documentation quality.
Hands-On Focus: Drafting internal notes, summaries, talking points, and formal communication with safe prompting.

Day 3: AI for Public Awareness and Scheme Communication
Description: Explores how AI can support government messaging, social content drafts, FAQ creation, script drafting, and campaign material development.
Hands-On Focus: Creating communication drafts for a public scheme in multiple formats and audience levels.

Day 4: AI for Synthetic Media Content Creation
Description: Introduces the use of AI-generated text, image, voice, and video for communication support, including low-cost content creation possibilities.
Hands-On Focus: Generating concept drafts, script outlines, and visual prompt ideas while discussing disclosure and misuse concerns.

Day 5: AI-Assisted Sketch and Prompt-to-Image Concepts
Description: Examines the idea of creating suspect or witness-assisted visual outputs using AI, while emphasizing that such outputs are assistive and must not be treated as unquestionable truth.
Hands-On Focus: Controlled prompt-to-image exercises focused on description quality, ambiguity, and limitations.

Day 6: Deepfake Awareness and Detection Fundamentals
Description: Covers what deepfakes are, how synthetic media manipulation works, what kinds of clues may indicate manipulation, and why human and technical review must both be used.
Hands-On Focus: Reviewing sample media characteristics, discussing detection limitations, and building a practical response checklist.

Day 7: AI in Cybersecurity and Threat Support
Description: Introduces AI support in phishing review, alert triage, anomaly detection, log summarization, intelligence assistance, and suspicious pattern analysis.
Hands-On Focus: Using AI to summarize cyber incidents, classify threat descriptions, and assist alert interpretation in a controlled learning setup.

Day 8: AI for Intelligence Support and Information Triage
Description: Focuses on how AI can help organize large amounts of text, identify patterns, extract signals, prioritize reading, and support analytical preparation without replacing expert judgment.
Hands-On Focus: Converting large unstructured text into structured intelligence-style notes and issue clusters.

Day 9: Operational Limitations and Red Lines in Sensitive Contexts
Description: Clarifies what kinds of tasks should not be delegated to AI without strong process safeguards. Includes discussion on evidence quality, legal defensibility, operational confidentiality, and misidentification risk.
Hands-On Focus: Scenario review in which the learner must decide whether AI may assist, must be limited, or must not be used.

Day 10: Public Sector and Cyber Context Capstone
Description: Integrates administrative, public communication, synthetic media, cyber, and analytical usage into a realistic scenario-based exercise.
Hands-On Focus: Full guided case from request intake to AI-assisted outputs, validation, escalation, and final human-reviewed decision package.

==================================================
PHASE 5 — GOVERN AND AUDIT AI
==================================================

Phase Purpose
This phase builds the learner’s ability to think like a responsible evaluator of AI systems. It prepares the learner to ask governance questions, identify control points, understand accountability, and build the mindset useful for AI governance and AI audit preparation.

Day 1: Introduction to AI Governance
Description: Explains why AI systems need governance and why capability alone is not enough. The learner is introduced to concepts such as accountability, oversight, transparency, and control.
Hands-On Focus: Reviewing example AI use cases and identifying where governance questions begin.

Day 2: AI Risk Identification
Description: Covers different categories of AI risk such as factual risk, bias risk, privacy risk, misuse risk, operational risk, reputational risk, and legal or public-interest risk.
Hands-On Focus: Creating a simple AI risk register for sample use cases.

Day 3: Controls, Guardrails, and Human Review
Description: Teaches how AI systems and AI-supported workflows should include process controls, role clarity, escalation paths, and human approval points.
Hands-On Focus: Designing a controlled workflow for a sensitive AI-assisted task.

Day 4: Data Governance and Data Quality for AI
Description: Explains why data quality, relevance, representativeness, traceability, and access control matter in AI systems and AI usage contexts.
Hands-On Focus: Evaluating fictional data scenarios to identify quality and governance weaknesses.

Day 5: Fairness, Bias, and Explainability in Governance Context
Description: Connects ethical AI principles with practical governance expectations, especially where decisions affect people, rights, public trust, or institutional legitimacy.
Hands-On Focus: Reviewing cases where biased or unexplained outputs would create governance failure.

Day 6: Documentation, Audit Trails, and Evidence of Oversight
Description: Covers the importance of maintaining records of prompts, source data, review decisions, validation steps, revisions, and approvals in sensitive AI-supported work.
Hands-On Focus: Building a simple documentation and audit-trace template for AI-assisted tasks.

Day 7: Evaluating AI Use Cases Before Adoption
Description: Teaches a practical decision framework for whether an AI use case should be adopted, piloted, restricted, or rejected based on value, risk, controls, and readiness.
Hands-On Focus: Scoring proposed use cases with a basic governance review matrix.

Day 8: AI Audit Mindset and Key Questions
Description: Introduces the mindset required for AI audit-oriented thinking, including questions about data, model behavior, control environment, traceability, accountability, and monitoring.
Hands-On Focus: Performing a guided audit-style review of sample AI workflows.

Day 9: Responsible AI in Government and Sensitive Institutions
Description: Applies governance thinking specifically to official environments where public trust, legal scrutiny, and high-consequence usage require stronger controls.
Hands-On Focus: Reviewing public-sector scenarios and defining governance requirements for each.

Day 10: Governance and Audit Readiness Capstone
Description: Concludes the course through a capstone that integrates governance, risk, controls, accountability, safe adoption, and audit-style evaluation.
Hands-On Focus: Complete review of an AI-supported workflow from proposal to risk analysis, controls design, documentation, and final recommendation.

==================================================
TOOLS AND LAB STRATEGY
==================================================

Primary Hands-On Tools
- ChatGPT
- Claude
- Gemini
- DeepSeek

Secondary Freemium / Mostly Free Support Tools
- Google Docs or Microsoft Word for output refinement and comparison
- Google Sheets or Excel for structured outputs and simple classification exercises
- NotebookLM or equivalent free research-support tools when accessible
- Free transcription / OCR / summarization utilities when needed for demonstration
- Free image-generation or image-analysis tools only where operationally appropriate

Tool Usage Principle
The course should rely first on browser-based tools that require minimal setup. Advanced paid tool usage should not be assumed. Any paid-only or API-based demonstration should be optional and shown only as awareness, not as a dependency for completion.

==================================================
DELIVERY REALISM NOTES
==================================================

1. This 10-day-per-phase plan is achievable because each phase is scoped around practical learning outcomes rather than excessive technical depth.
2. The learner is not being trained as a machine learning engineer; the plan is designed for strategic, operational, and governance-oriented fluency with strong hands-on practice.
3. The most time-sensitive challenge will be Phase 4, because sensitive-context use cases require careful explanation, boundaries, and discussion.
4. If needed, lightweight homework can be added after each class in the form of 15 to 20 minute prompt practice or output-review exercises.
5. The course is professional-level because it moves beyond generic tool demonstrations and instead builds capability, judgment, workflow discipline, and governance maturity.

==================================================
EXPECTED LEARNING OUTCOME AFTER ALL 5 PHASES
==================================================

By the end of the full program, the learner should be able to understand what modern AI is, use major free / freemium AI tools effectively for professional work, validate and critique outputs, apply AI thoughtfully in government and cyber-relevant contexts, and assess AI usage through the lens of governance, controls, and audit readiness.
